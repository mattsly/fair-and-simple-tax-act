# LinkedIn Post Draft

It's that time of year. March Madness 🏀🎉...but also tax season 💸😭

So while checking our brackets, many of us will also collect our forms and wade through provisions and watch the tax software flip between red and green like a slot machine. Or we'll pay someone a lot of money to...basically do an absurdly complex form data entry. And we'll still not be really sure we did it right.

What a terrible "product" experience.

I've spent the past month working through the following: "what would a ground-up refactor of the U.S. tax system actually look like?"

I'm starting with capital gains reform, and I put together the following proposal. It's radical, but also pragmatic. There are pieces the political right and left will each like (and not like). I've gotten promising early feedback from policy researchers and even a few politicians. I think it's pretty elegant, if I do say so myself.

It is based on five simple rules. (note: the specific numbers are stakes in the ground — the structural framework matters more than the exact calibration):

1. Every American gets a $2.5M lifetime capital gains exemption ($5M married). Any source, any asset type, no qualification tests.
2. Above the exemption, rates scale smoothly up to the top income tax rate at $10M in lifetime gains, after which gains and income are pegged at the same rate.
3. You can't defer gains forever. Sale, death, gift, and borrowing all trigger realization. This closes "buy, borrow, die" and eliminates stepped-up basis.
4. Basis is indexed to CPI. You're taxed on real gains, not inflation.
5. Roth reforms: remove the income cap, raise the annual limit, cap the balance at $5M. No more Thiel loophole.

From these five rules, roughly 10-15% of the tax code becomes unnecessary:

- No more AMT, NIIT, or estate tax (hear me out!)
- No need for a wealth tax
- Cut various provisions: QSBS (hear me out! again!), Section 121, 1031, etc
- Carried interest debate becomes moot
- $85-200B in annual deficit reduction

We can recapture all of the tax compliance deadweight loss that our economy endures very year. And we can watch a little more March Madness.

https://www.mattsly.com/fair-and-simple-tax-act/lifetime-gains-essay

5. roth reforms - eliminate income cap, cap balance (eliminate thiel loophole)

- no more AMT, NIIT
