# What Are We Optimizing For?

**Status:** Working draft — potential future companion piece or appendix for academic audiences

Any algorithm is designed to maximize a specific output. If we rewrite the tax code, we must be explicit about our optimization function. We are not optimizing purely for wealth equalization, nor are we optimizing for raw, unguided GDP growth.

We are optimizing for **Dynamic Mobility** and **Resource Velocity**. Both are measurable.

## 1. Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs
We uniformly meet, with 99.9% probability, the level 1 needs (physiological: clean air, sufficient safe food, clean water, shelter, warmth and a place to sleep) along with physical safety needs from level 2. physical safety includes free, quality healthcare for catastrophic bad luck (accidents, cancer)

**How we measure it:** Each of these statistics, we track to 99.9% probability that on a given day, a citizen will have access to all of these every day

## 2. Dynamic Mobility
This is the "Einstein Rubric": the system should be architected so that anyone, regardless of birth, can rise to their potential. A healthy economy is a fluid one where talent flows upward and stagnation sinks.

**How we measure it:** We track Intergenerational Mobility — the probability that a child born into the bottom quintile reaches the top. We assume that talent is uniformly distributed (? is still a safe assumption) so that we want the probability that someone in the top quintile to be entirely uncorrelated with the quintile into which they are born.


## 3. Pie Expansion
Resources are allocated so that our society creates value over time. We want to continue to be the country that developes new technologies that increase the overall wealth of the country

**How we measure it:** GDP grow(??)


## 4. Resource Velocity

Resource velocity is the speed at which capital moves out of stagnant legacy shelters and into productive ventures.

**How we measure it:** We track the Realization Ratio — the ratio of realized gains to unrealized gains among the top 1% of households. Currently, billionaires sit on mountains of "dead" unrealized wealth. We also track Average Asset Holding Periods; a drop in these periods suggests capital is being redeployed based on market fundamentals rather than tax avoidance timelines.

**The Goal:** Rule 3 (Terminal Realization) acts as a systemic "tree-shaker," forcing stagnant resources out of the shadows and back into the arena where they can fund the next breakthrough.

## 5. Systemic Legitimacy

Finally, we optimize for the "Social Contract." A system is only durable if it is perceived as legitimate by those within it.

**How we measure it:** We track Effective Tax Rate (ETR) Parity. The framework is successful when the effective tax rate of a billionaire investor matches the effective tax rate of a highly compensated surgeon or engineer. We also track NPS of taxpayer's feeling about being an American.
