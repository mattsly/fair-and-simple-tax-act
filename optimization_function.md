# What Are We Optimizing For?

**Status:** Working draft — potential future companion piece or appendix for academic audiences

Any algorithm is designed to maximize a specific output. If we rewrite the tax code, we must be explicit about our optimization function. We are not optimizing purely for wealth equalization, nor are we optimizing for raw, unguided GDP growth.

We are optimizing for **Dynamic Mobility** and **Resource Velocity**. Both are measurable.

## 1. Dynamic Mobility

This is the "Einstein Rubric": the system should be architected so that anyone, regardless of birth, can rise to their potential. A healthy economy is a fluid one where talent flows upward and stagnation sinks.

**How we measure it:** We track Intergenerational Mobility — the probability that a child born into the bottom quintile reaches the top. We also track the Seed-Stage Capital Ratio: the volume of capital flowing to first-time, non-institutional founders compared to the volume of late-stage "safe" bets.

**The Goal:** By protecting the first $2.5 million of gain (Rule 1), we explicitly subsidize the rising innovator while ensuring that unearned, inherited wealth is eventually exposed to the system (Rule 3).

## 2. Resource Velocity

Resource velocity is the speed at which capital moves out of stagnant legacy shelters and into productive, high-risk ventures.

**How we measure it:** We track the Realization Ratio — the ratio of realized gains to unrealized gains among the top 1% of households. Currently, billionaires sit on mountains of "dead" unrealized wealth. We also track Average Asset Holding Periods; a drop in these periods suggests capital is being redeployed based on market fundamentals rather than tax avoidance timelines.

**The Goal:** Rule 3 (Terminal Realization) acts as a systemic "tree-shaker," forcing stagnant resources out of the shadows and back into the arena where they can fund the next breakthrough.

## 3. Systemic Legitimacy

Finally, we optimize for the "Social Contract." A system is only durable if it is perceived as legitimate by those within it.

**How we measure it:** We track Effective Tax Rate (ETR) Parity. The framework is successful when the effective tax rate of a billionaire investor matches the effective tax rate of a highly compensated surgeon or engineer.

**The Goal:** By irrevocably linking capital gains to the top income tax rate (Rule 2), we end the era of special carve-outs. We ensure the investor class and the labor class are playing the same game, with the same rules, and the same stake in the outcome.
