# Hero Image Spec: "The Magic Gemstone"

## Purpose
Social sharing image (og:image) and Substack hero for an essay called "The Magic Gemstone: A thought experiment about the tax code we'd design if we could start over."

## Dimensions
- Primary: 1200x630px (Open Graph / Substack hero)
- Secondary: 1200x1200px (square crop for social)

## Concept

A single glowing gemstone (think large, uncut, slightly rough, radiant) sitting on a plain white marble surface in a spare, official-looking room. The room should evoke "government building" — think columns, marble, maybe a faint suggestion of the Treasury or a congressional chamber — but abstracted, not literal. Clean, simple, almost empty.

The gemstone itself is the focal point. It should be luminous, maybe emerald or sapphire-colored, casting light onto the marble surface. It should feel magical but grounded — not fantasy-genre, more "what if this actually existed in the real world." Think of how a museum might display a singular, important artifact.

**Key visual tension:** The gemstone is radiant and beautiful, but there should be a subtle crack or fracture running through it. Maybe a faint wisp of smoke rising from the crack. The idea is that this thing is about to break. It hasn't combusted yet, but you can tell something is wrong.

## Mood
- Clean, institutional, slightly austere
- The gemstone is the only source of warmth/color in the frame
- NOT fantasy or sci-fi aesthetic
- NOT political cartoon or editorial illustration style
- Think: a New Yorker cover meets a Bloomberg Businessweek photo illustration

## Text Overlay (if included)
- Title: "The Magic Gemstone"
- Subtitle: "A thought experiment about taxes"
- Author: "Matt Sly"
- Font: clean sans-serif (similar to the site's existing typography)
- Placement: lower-left or lower-right, not competing with the gemstone
- Text should be optional — the image should work with or without it

## What to Avoid
- No dollar signs, tax forms, IRS logos, or other literal tax imagery
- No cartoon style or clip art
- No American flags or overtly patriotic symbols
- No people
- No busy backgrounds or complex compositions
- No dark/moody lighting — this should feel clean and bright

## Color Palette
- Mostly white/marble/grey (the room)
- One saturated color (the gemstone — emerald green, deep sapphire blue, or rich amber)
- Warm light cast from the gemstone onto the neutral surface

## Reference Tone
Think of the Bloomberg Businessweek school of editorial imagery: conceptual, minimal, slightly surreal, high production value. Or the kind of illustration The Economist might commission — smart, clean, one central visual metaphor.
