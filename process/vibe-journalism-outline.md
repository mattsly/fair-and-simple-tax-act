# Vibe Journalism (working title)
*Draft outline — brainstormed April 2026*

## Working thesis
Everyone is vibe coding. I'm trying vibe journalism. AI democratized production but not distribution — and that's the part nobody's talking about.

## Hook
Launching a startup and launching a policy idea feel surprisingly similar. Have an idea. Ship early and often. Iterate. And then discover that distribution was always the hard part.

## Section 1: Creation got cheaper (and this is real)
- The vibe coding parallel: non-programmers building apps, non-journalists writing substantive pieces
- What AI actually enables: faster iteration, test arguments before you publish, explore edge cases, catch holes early — the ideas can be *better*
- Personal: my tax reform framework would have taken years without AI; now it's months
- **Meta moment:** this essay is itself vibe journalism, written with Claude. Don't hedge it — lean in.

## Section 2: But distribution is the same hard problem
- An app on your laptop isn't a startup
- A post in a Google Doc isn't journalism
- Getting a Globe op-ed placed, building a Substack audience, reaching a senator's staffer — AI didn't touch any of that
- The startup parallel holds exactly: product-market fit is necessary but not sufficient

## Section 3: The gotchas the hype skips
- Domain expertise still required — AI confidently wrong is worse than a blank page
- Credibility doesn't come from polish — one bad number and you're dismissed
- The viber is accountable for the output regardless of who helped build it
- Deployment is a real step (both for apps and posts)

## Landing
The democratization is real but narrow. What got cheaper was production. What didn't get cheaper: judgment, distribution, credibility. Those were always the job.

## Open questions / things to develop
- What's the right title? "Vibe Journalism" is punchy but might read as self-deprecating
- How much to reveal about the AI-assisted process? Full transparency vs. letting the work speak
- Audience: tech-adjacent readers who get the vibe coding reference, vs. broader Substack audience who may not
