# Infographic Specs for AI Image Generator

## Option A: The Sliding Scale (Recommended)

Create a clean, modern infographic with a white or very light gray background. No clip art, no illustrations of people, no flags. This should look like something from The Economist or a McKinsey report, not a political flyer.

**Title** (top, bold, dark gray): "The Lifetime Gains Framework: How It Works"

**Main visual**: A single chart taking up ~60% of the image.

X-axis: "Cumulative Lifetime Capital Gains" — labeled at $0, $2.5M, $5M, $7.5M, $10M
Y-axis: "Tax Rate on Next Dollar of Gains" — labeled at 0%, 10%, 20%, 30%, 37%

The chart shows a line that:
- Is flat at 0% from $0 to $2.5M (this zone is shaded light green and labeled "TAX-FREE ZONE — 98% of Americans never leave this zone")
- Rises linearly from 0% at $2.5M to 37% at $10M (this zone is shaded light yellow/amber and labeled "Sliding Scale")
- Is flat at 37% from $10M onward (this zone is shaded light red/coral and labeled "Ordinary Income Rate")

A horizontal dashed line at 23.8% is labeled "Current Law: Flat rate for everyone" — this shows where the current system sits relative to the new one.

**Below the chart**, three compact callout boxes in a horizontal row:

Box 1 (green accent): "Below $2.5M" / "0% tax rate" / "Replaces Section 121, QSBS, and 10 other exclusions"
Box 2 (amber accent): "$2.5M – $10M" / "0% → 37% sliding scale" / "Smooth phase-in eliminates bunching penalty"
Box 3 (red accent): "Above $10M" / "37% (ordinary income rate)" / "Capital gains and income rates converge"

**Footer** (small, gray text): "The Lifetime Gains Framework — mattsly.com" and "Note: $2.5M and $10M thresholds are proposed starting points, indexed to CPI"

**Style notes for the generator:**
- Color palette: white background, dark gray text, green/amber/red accents only
- Font style: clean sans-serif (like Inter, Helvetica, or similar)
- No gradients, no drop shadows, no 3D effects
- No illustrations, icons, or clip art
- Data-forward, minimal, professional
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 (landscape) for LinkedIn/Twitter sharing

## Option B: Who Wins, Who Loses (Comparison Table)

Create a clean, modern infographic with a white background. No clip art or illustrations.

**Title** (top, bold, dark gray): "The Lifetime Gains Framework: Who Pays What"

**Subtitle** (smaller, gray): "Comparing current law to five simple rules"

**Main visual**: A horizontal bar chart or comparison table showing 6 scenarios, each with two bars (Current Law in gray, Framework in blue/green):

| Scenario | Current Law | Framework |
|---|---|---|
| Homeowner ($250K gain) | $0 | $0 |
| Tech employee ($1.2M, 15-year career) | $285K | $0 |
| First-time founder ($2.8M exit) | $555K | ~$2K |
| Successful founder ($19M exit) | $4.5M | $4.7M |
| Inherited estate ($15M unrealized) | $0 | $3.2M |
| Billionaire buy-borrow-die ($5M/yr) | $0 | $1.85M/yr |

Use green highlighting or a downward arrow for scenarios where the framework saves money. Use red/neutral for scenarios where it costs more. The visual contrast should make the pattern immediately obvious: everyone below ~$17.5M pays less; the revenue comes from inherited wealth and buy-borrow-die.

**Below the table**, one line: "98% of Americans pay the same or less. Revenue comes from closing loopholes, not raising rates."

**Footer**: "The Lifetime Gains Framework — mattsly.com"

**Style notes**: Same as Option A — clean, minimal, data-forward, 16:9 landscape.
